Chicken Noodle Soup

Not a recipe, a process

I make chicken noodle soup regularly, and it is one of my favorite hobbies. Perfecting the broth, finding the little tweaks to the seasoning and herb, the soup ingredients - there is a lot of depth to what most people see as a simple meal they probably haven't had since they were a kid.

As it is an ongoing process, and I wouldn't say I'm a professional chef, this cannot be a recipe (yet). This is a description of the process I use every time I go grocery shopping to produce ridiculously good and healthy meals for the next few nights using relatively little money and time investment.

Hardware

  • 2 tall soup pots, appropriately sized to your stove burners
  • Ladle
  • Fat skimmer with a fine mesh
  • Wooden spoon, or other stirring implement
  • Serving bowl(s)

Herbs and seasoning

  • Black and pink peppercorns
  • Dried or fresh herbs
    • Oregano
    • Rosemary
    • Thyme
    • Parsley
    • Cilantro
    • Sage
    • Bay leaves
  • Seasoning
    • Smoked paprika
    • Cracked pepper
    • Salt
    • MSG
  • Red Wine Vinegar
  • Olive oil

Soup ingredients

  • Carrots
  • Celery stalks - outer for the broth, inner for the soup
  • Onion, any kind
  • Peas or corn
  • Chicken meat
  • Noodles, any kind
  • Spring onion, for garnish
